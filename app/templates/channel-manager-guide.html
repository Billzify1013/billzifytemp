What Is a Channel Manager and Why Do Indian Hotels Need One?

A channel manager is software that connects your hotel to multiple Online Travel Agencies (OTAs) — like MakeMyTrip, Booking.com, Airbnb, Goibibo, and Agoda — and keeps your room availability and pricing automatically synced in real time across all of them.

Without a channel manager, every time a booking comes in from one OTA, you must manually update your availability on every other platform. This takes time, and the window of 15–30 minutes between a booking and manual update is enough for another booking to come in on a different platform — causing a double booking.

68%
of Indian hoteliers who manage OTAs manually experience at least one double booking per month — directly damaging their reviews and reputation.

The Problem With Manual OTA Management

Most small and mid-size hotel owners in India list their property on 3 to 5 OTAs. Managing these manually means:

  • Logging into each platform separately — MakeMyTrip, Booking.com, Goibibo, Agoda, Airbnb — every single day
  • Updating room availability manually after each booking, which takes 15–45 minutes per update
  • Risk of human error — forgetting to close a room on one platform leads to double bookings
  • Inconsistent pricing — different rates on different platforms confuse guests and hurt your ranking on each OTA
  • Revenue loss from empty rooms — because updating is slow, rooms appear unavailable even when they are free
💡 Did You Know?

Hotels using a channel manager fill 23% more rooms on average because their inventory is always accurate and live across all platforms — even at 3am.

How a Channel Manager Works (Simple Explanation)

Think of a channel manager as your central control room. You set your rooms, rates, and availability once — in the channel manager dashboard. It then pushes this information live to every OTA simultaneously.

When a booking arrives from any OTA, the channel manager instantly removes that room from availability on all other platforms. There is zero delay, zero manual work, and zero double bookings.
